A poem for Maths Teacher

16 Oct, 2021

You brought numbers into our lives Alphabets you knew 6 and how I wish they weren’t algebraic Logs were tricky and decimals weren’t fun
but solved complex equations only for U Problems you had infinite from the book or life With solutions you nurtured logic and common sense Trigonometry was monotony until you came up with geometry. Oh! And I Never knew shapes would be this important
Until I met the gym coach He’s a constant remainder for size zero.

I might not remember all the math you taught I definetly don’t use all the math you taught But I still owe you a tonne not because you brought numbers into our lives But only because you breathed your life into us in our prime More true now that Binary and Boolean
